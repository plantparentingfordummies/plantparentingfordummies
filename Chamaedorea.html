Chamaedorea or Parlour Palm

Make new fronds!

Origins: Native to Guatemala and Mexico but used as indoor plants since Victorian era in their parlours, hence the name

Light: They can tolerate low light, but seeing that its native to warm countries, they are optimal at medium shade. They don’t enjoy direct light so if its a warm, sunny room, please place the palm well away from the window

Water: Would need a lot of water, so water weekly or more in Summer if the top layer of soil is dry. It will be good to keep the palm moist by misting once a week to keep the leaves green and fresh

Soil and Repotting: They are one of the slowest growing plants and will take over 10 years to reach full height and potential, so keep and eye on the bottom of the pot for exposed roots every year

Food: It’s a slow grower, and will appreciate a feed liquid of fertiliser once every month in spring and summer

Fancy Quality: Very High - Your guests will be very impressed!
