Cactus Eurphobia

Free hugs anyone?

Origins: This type of leaf growing cactus is native to Southern Africa

Light: Loves those warm rays, so feel free to put them in the warmest, brightest spots. In winter, keep in a warm dry spot

Water: Really low maintenance with water, given that they are used to desert conditions. Before the next watering session, make sure the cactus has fully dried out, then lightly water

Soil and Repotting: They grow thick and tall so as it grows, it will need a sturdy pot with a good soil mix, that wonâ€™t tip over as the cactus gets bigger

Food: They need cactus specific feed as the soils are not the same. Dilute the feed to half-strength then feed once every two months in spring and summer

Pet/Baby Safe: 0/10 - if you are a parent to a baby or a pet, then this one is not for you as these cacti produce a milky sap that can cause rashes on skin or can be highly toxic if ingested
